Yungang Grottoes
The Yungang Grottoes are located at the southern foot of Wuzhou Mountain, 17 kilometers west of Datong City, Shanxi Province, northern China. The grottoes were carved into the mountainside and extend one kilometer from east to west. They contain 45 major caves, 252 niches of various sizes, and more than 51,000 stone sculptures, making them one of the largest ancient grotto complexes in China. Together with the Mogao Grottoes in Dunhuang, the Longmen Grottoes in Luoyang, and the Maijishan Grottoes in Tianshui, they are acclaimed as the four great treasuries of grotto art in China. In 1961, they were announced by the State Council as one of the first batch of nationally important cultural heritage sites. On December 14, 2001, they were inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List. On May 8, 2007, they were rated by the National Tourism Administration as one of the first batch of national 5A-level tourist attractions.


Yungang Five Flower Caves
Caves 9 through 13, located in the central section of the Yungang Grottoes, are collectively known as the Five Flower Caves. They earned this name during the Qing Dynasty when they were decorated with painted murals depicting the scenery of the Yungang Grottoes. The Five Flower Caves are richly and elaborately decorated, serving as precious historical material for the study of Northern Wei history, art, music, dance, calligraphy, and architecture, and are an important part of the Yungang Grotto complex.

Pagoda Caves
The eastern cluster of the Yungang Grottoes, referring to Caves 1 through 4 at the eastern end, are all pagoda caves. Caves 1 and 2 were excavated together as a pair before Emperor Xiaowen relocated the capital to Luoyang. In the center of each cave stands a square pagoda pillar with niches and statues carved on all four sides. The main statue in Cave 1 is Maitreya. The south face of the pagoda has Shakyamuni and Prabhutaratna carved on the lower tier and Shakyamuni on the upper tier. The five-tiered miniature pagoda carved in relief serves as an important visual reference for studying Northern Wei architecture. Cave 2 features a Shakyamuni statue; the south face of its pagoda has Shakyamuni and Prabhutaratna on the lower tier and the Three Buddhas of the Three Ages on the upper tier. Both caves have Vimalakirti and Manjushri carved on either side of the south-wall entrance. Cave 3 is the largest cave in the entire Yungang complex; its front wall rises to approximately 25 meters and is traditionally said to have been the sutra-translation tower of the monk Tanyao.


Wuzhou Mountain
Wuzhou Mountain, also known as Wuzhou Mountain, lies among the hills west of Datong City. The Song Dynasty text Taiping Huanyu Ji, citing the Ji Prefecture Map, records: "Wuzhou Mountain is located to the northwest of the prefecture, extending several hundred li from east to west and fifty li from north to south. Its southern face rises in a sheer cliff of a thousand ren." The Yungang Grottoes were carved by cutting into the southern edge of Wuzhou Mountain.

Tanyao Five Caves
Caves 16 through 20 are the five earliest caves excavated at the Yungang Grottoes, collectively known as the "Tanyao Five Caves." Cave 16 has an oval floor plan. The principal image at the center is a standing Shakyamuni, 13.5 meters tall, standing on a lotus pedestal, with the surrounding walls carved with a thousand Buddhas and niches. Cave 17's principal images are the Three Buddhas of the Three Ages; the central figure is a seated Maitreya in meditation, 15.6 meters tall. Niches are carved on both the east and west walls — a seated figure on the east and a standing figure on the west. The Buddhist niche on the east side of the bright window, bearing an inscription dated to the 13th year of the Taihe era of the Northern Wei (489 CE), was added at a later time.

Location
The Yungang Grottoes are located at the southern foot of Wuzhou Mountain, 17 kilometers west of Datong City, Shanxi Province, northern China.

Transportation:

Public buses and tourist coaches are available from outside the West Gate of Datong City. From Datong Railway Station, take Bus No. 4 (1 yuan, approximately 30 minutes, via Xima Road - Dabei Street - Daxi Street; taxi fare is approximately 8 yuan) to Xinkaili Bus Station in the western part of the city, then transfer to Bus No. 3 (1.5 yuan, approximately 30 minutes) directly to the East Gate of the Yungang scenic area. You can also take a minibus from beside the Hongqi Grand Hotel heading toward Yanshan, and alight mid-route for 5 yuan.
